NYCDOT Plaza Graphics and Webmap


I made these graphics to support a plaza research initiative while working at the NYC DOT's Public Space Unit as an intern. Our intern team developed a short survey form and collected data on several of NYC DOT’s plazas. I conducted data analysis using R statistics and put together reports on the findings.

The first spread is a map I made to support the plaza research initiative to bring some context to the statistics. I used ArcGIS to compose a map that shows selected land uses within ¼ mile from the edge of each plaza. The yellow plaza boundaries are sourced from a shapefile I produced. We needed CAD drawings of all the 73 (planned and existing) plazas in the program at the moment, so it was suggested we should also make a plaza boundary shapefile. I exported a shapefile with roadbeds and building footprint into CAD, and our team then drew the plaza outlines on CAD using existing documentation as guidance. When all the drawing was done, I imported the CAD file back into GIS and the shapefile was implemented into a DOT webmap.

The second spread shows some key statistics for each plaza and a graph that I put together to help visualize the plaza survey data. I ran ANOVA and mosaic plots on the data and used the graphic on the bottom right to visualize parts of the data that stood out statistically. These were then considered to be defining characteristics of each plaza. The difference in size of the icons is proportional to the difference in frequency. The graphic visualizes how long respondents reported usually staying at the plaza, what means of transport they used to get there and what was their relationship to the surrounding neighborhood.
