This is a selection of environmental and urban planning graphics that I developed while working at AKRF.

This is the type of quick and dirty spatial analysis that we sometimes need to develop on the fly. The Economics group needed quick ballpark estimates of ridership for New Jersey Transit rail lines at Census block group level. I first created Voronoi (Thiessen) polygons to estimate "catchment areas" for each station. A block group that has its centroid within the catchment area of a station was assigned to that station (color coded in the figure). This also means that the majority of that block group falls within that catchment area polygon for all but the most oddly shaped block groups â€” close enough for the purpose of this analysis. I was then able to select the relevant block groups within different study area boundaries from the stations and provide ridership estimates for each station using U.S. Census LODES data.
